Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

The concept of neuroplasticity is one worth understanding as a learner. The brain has the potential to develop its structure and capacity positively and people who understand this can help it by treating their mind and body well. Maintaining good bodily and mental health is all about good habits. Things like exercise, diet, sleep and learning all come together to help brain plasticity and support a person’s overall health.

Supporting one’s neuroplasticity is best done through conscious action. To support my learning its best to set real goals and consciously focus on achieving them, the overall goal being to turn these smaller goals into habits. Also, worth noting is the fact that if things don’t go completely according the set plans and the goals are not achieved, it’s important to not get discouraged and let it derail progress made in making or maintaining good habits.

This post about neuroplasticity by Peter Barnes has an excellent infographic detailing the concept of neuroplasticity.



Growth Mindset

The idea of neuroplasticity is also connected to research about growth and fixed mindsets. The way I see it, mindsets work as a self-fulfilling prophecy. People tend to not aim any higher than the believe themselves capable. Someone who thinks their potential is fixed and their brain just doesn’t have the capacity to learn a lot aren’t going to try. Those who recognize the plasticity of their brain and understand that they can develop and achieve great things will aim higher and achieve higher. Through conscious thought and action, I can support the neuroplasticity of my brain and maintain a positive perspective on my ability to achieve highly.

This article by Carol Dweck has an excellent explanation of the thoughts and actions that go into creating and maintaining a growth mindset.
